Dana Point was designated a Whale Heritage Site by the World Cetacean Alliance in 2021, making it one of only four locations on Earth with that certification at the time[^1]. The city trademarked the title "Dolphin and Whale Watching Capital of the World" in 2019[^2], and it hosts the longest-running Festival of Whales in the world, held continuously since 1971[^3]. The waters off the headlands sit above a submarine canyon that funnels nutrient-rich upwelling close to shore, concentrating marine life within sight of the bluffs.

> **At a glance:** Year-round whale and dolphin sightings from the harbor, a Whale Heritage Site designation shared with only three other places globally (as of 2021), and a megamouth shark caught here in 1990 that was one of the earliest specimens science had ever seen.

## Whale Heritage Site

The World Cetacean Alliance's Whale Heritage Site certification recognizes locations where cetaceans (whales and dolphins) are embraced through the cultural, economic, social, and political lives of communities, and where people and cetaceans coexist in what the alliance calls an "authentic and respectful way." Prior to Dana Point's 2021 designation, only three other locations worldwide held this certification[^1].

The designation reflects decades of whale-watching infrastructure built around the harbor. Multiple operators run daily trips year-round from Dana Point Harbor. Blue whales pass through in summer, gray whales migrate in winter and spring, and pods of common dolphins numbering in the hundreds are visible most days of the year.

## The Megamouth and the Great Whites

In 1990, one of the few known specimens of the megamouth shark was caught off Dana Point[^4]. At the time, the species had been discovered only thirteen years earlier (1976) and fewer than a dozen had ever been recorded. The Dana Point specimen was significant because so little was known about the animal's range and behavior.

Juvenile great white sharks sometimes congregate in the nearshore waters. They feed primarily on fish in the kelp beds located just offshore rather than targeting larger prey. The kelp beds also make Dana Point a destination for snorkelers, spearfishers, and recreational fishermen.

## Festival of Whales

The Festival of Whales has been held since 1971 (some sources cite 1972 for the formal inaugural event). It runs over two weekends in March, coinciding with the gray whale migration. The festival includes harbor cruises, street fairs, and educational programs run in partnership with the Ocean Institute at the harbor.

## Watching from Shore

The high cliffs at Dana Point provide natural vantage points for scanning the horizon. Whale spouts are visible from the bluff trails on calm days, particularly during the gray whale migration (December through April). The Headlands trail offers the highest unobstructed views. Binoculars help, but spouts from gray whales within a half-mile of shore are visible to the naked eye.
